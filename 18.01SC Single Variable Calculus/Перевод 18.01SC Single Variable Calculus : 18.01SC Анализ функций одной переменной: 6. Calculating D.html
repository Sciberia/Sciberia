In other words, the derivative of the sum of two functions is just the sum of their derivatives. We’ll now prove that this is true for any pair of functions $u$ and $v$, provided that those functions have derivatives.

Since we don’t know in advance what functions $u$ and $v$ are, we can’t use any specific information about the functions or the slopes of their graphs; all we have to work with is the formal definition of the derivative.

When we apply the definition of the derivative to the function $(u + v)(x)$ we get: $$ (u + v)'(x) = \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} \frac{(u + v)(x + \Delta x) − (u + v)(x)}{\Delta x} $$

#13

Применим определение производной к функции $(u+v) (x)$:
$$(u+v)'(x) = \lim_{\Delta x\to0} \frac{(u+v)(x+\Delta x)-(u+v)(x)}{\Delta x}$$

Since $(u + v)(x)$ is just $u(x)$ + $v(x)$, $$(u + v)' (x) = \lim_{Δx→0} \frac{u(x + Δx) + v(x+Δx)-u(x)-v(x)}{Δx}$$

#14

Поскольку $(u+v)(x)$ - это просто $u(x) + v(x)$,
$$(u+v)'(x) = \lim_{Δx→0} \frac{u(x + Δx) + v(x+Δx)-u(x)-v(x)}{Δx}$$

Combining like terms, we see that: $$\lim_{Δx \to 0} \frac{u(x+Δx)-u(x)+v(x+Δx)-v(x)}{Δx}$$ or $$\lim_{Δx \to 0} \left[ \frac{u(x+Δx)-u(x)}{Δx} + \frac{v(x+Δx)-v(x)}{Δx} \right]$$

#15

Комбинируя, получаем:
$$ (u+v)'(x) = \lim_{Δx \to 0} \frac{u(x+Δx)-u(x)+v(x+Δx)-v(x)}{Δx} $$
или
$$(u+v)'(x) = \lim_{Δx \to 0} \left[ \frac{u(x+Δx)-u(x)}{Δx} + \frac{v(x+Δx)-v(x)}{Δx} \right]$$

Because u and v are differentiable (and therefore continuous), the limit of the sum is the sum of the limits. Therefore:
$$(u+v)'(x) = \lim_{Δx \to 0} \frac{u(x+Δx) - u(x)}{Δx} + \lim_{Δx \to 0} \frac{v(x+Δx)-v(x)}{Δx}$$

#16

Поскольку $u$ и $v$ дифференцируемы (и, следовательно, непрерывны), а предел суммы есть сумма пределов, получаем:
$$(u+v)'(x) = \lim_{Δx \to 0} \frac{u(x+Δx) - u(x)}{Δx} + \lim_{Δx \to 0} \frac{v(x+Δx)-v(x)}{Δx}$$

The two limits above match the definition of the derivatives of $u$ and $v$, so we’ve shown that $(u+v)'(x) = u'(x) + v'(x)$.

#17

Два предела выше совпадают с определением производных $u$ и $v$, так что мы показали, что $(u+v)'(x) = u'(x) + v'(x)$.
